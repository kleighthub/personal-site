Babylist · 2023

Wishlist

Evolved Babylist from a mono-list product to a multi-list, multi-occasion platform. Led a team of 11 from discovery through launch.

RoleDirector of Product
Team11 people
TimelineQ4 2022 – Oct 2023
Outcome$38.1M in incremental purchase value

01 · Discovery

Is there a market?

46% of holiday shoppers create a wishlist, and 79% of past Babylist users said they'd use it as one — early signal that demand reached well beyond baby registries.

02 · UXR

Defining the problem

Mapping the full gifting journey showed people stitching together Amazon, notes apps, and spreadsheets. No single tool covered the job from planning through follow-up.

03 · Shaping

Sticky notes vs. saved search

Two directions competed early on. The one that won: photo search — snap a picture of an item and get matching products back instantly.

“Now that I have it, I don't want it taken away.”
Lina, beta tester

04 · Beta Test

9 families, 6 weeks

Three had never used Babylist before. People loved linking multiple retailers in one place — but the experience still felt too baby-heavy for a birthday or holiday list.

05 · Iteration

Back to the drawing board

Three days to reshape the solution: let people share a child's interests directly, and make the feed feel less baby-specific.

06 · Launch

Celebrating (& debugging)

Live in time for the holidays — birthday, Christmas, and Hanukkah lists shipped alongside the usual baby registries, bugs and all.
